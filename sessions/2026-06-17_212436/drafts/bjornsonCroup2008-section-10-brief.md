# Content Brief — Section 10: Pharmacotherapy — Other Drugs

**Lines:** 79–82 (per outline)

## What to capture

No controlled trials support use of these drug categories in croup.

### Analgesics / antipyretics

- Reasonable for reduction of fever or discomfort
- No RCT evidence, but widely accepted as appropriate symptomatic support

### Antibiotics

- Most croup is viral; superinfections (bacterial tracheitis, pneumonia) are rare (<1 per 1000 croup cases)
- Routine prophylactic antibiotics are NOT reasonable given rarity of bacterial complications

### Antitussives and decongestants

- No physiologically rational basis for use in upper-airway obstruction disease
- **Should NOT be administered**

### Short-acting β2-agonists

- No clear rationale for use in upper-airway (not lower-airway) disease
- **Should NOT be administered**

## Suggested sub-bullets

- These drugs are sometimes used despite no evidence (practice variation documented in 21 Alberta hospitals)
- Key distinction: croup is an upper-airway disease (not lower-airway bronchospasm), which explains why β2-agonists are not indicated
- Emphasis on established therapies (corticosteroids, epinephrine) vs unproven adjuncts
