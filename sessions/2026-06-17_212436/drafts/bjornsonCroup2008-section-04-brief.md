# Content Brief — Section 04: Assessment of Severity

**Lines:** 49–53 (per outline)

## What to capture

- Determination of disease severity relies on **clinical assessment**
- Various proposed objective assessment methods (clinical scores) are either **impractical** or **insensitive** across the full range of severity
- In clinical trials: outcome measures mainly include clinical scores and health-care use
- Such scores are useful for research but **none has been shown to enhance routine clinical care** (not reliable when used by wide range of clinicians)
- Features useful in routine clinical assessment are outlined in the management algorithm (Figure in the paper)
  - Mild: no stridor or significant chest-wall indrawing at rest → may discharge home
  - Moderate: stridor and chest-wall indrawing at rest without agitation
  - Severe: stridor and indrawing of the sternum associated with agitation or lethargy

## Suggested sub-bullets

- The management algorithm (referenced from Johnson D et al., Alberta Medical Association clinical practice guidelines) operationalises severity assessment
- Practical clinical features used to assess severity: stridor at rest, chest-wall indrawing, level of agitation, presence of lethargy
- Note: severity assessment is integral to the treatment algorithm (corticosteroids, epinephrine, observation, admission decisions flow from severity)
