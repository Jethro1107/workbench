# Humidified Air

- **Ineffectiveness** - Treatment of croup with humidified air is not effective, despite its long history of use; humidification of air is neither completely benign nor does it improve respiratory distress
- **Evidence: systematic review** - Three RCTs (135 children with mild-to-moderate croup) in emergency settings found no difference in croup score after humidified air treatment
- **Evidence: later RCT** - 140 children with moderate-to-severe croup, three arms: traditional standard humidified blow-by oxygen; 40% humidified oxygen; or 100% humidified oxygen with particle size generated to target the larynx; no difference in croup score, treatment with epinephrine or dexamethasone, or admission to hospital or additional medical care between groups
- **Placebo arm revealed** - Measurement of humidified blow-by oxygen showed it did not raise humidity above ambient room air, thus effectively serving as a placebo arm
- **Potential harms** - Several identified:
    - Hot humidified air → scald injuries
    - Mist tents → disperse fungus and moulds unless properly cleaned
    - Mist tents are cold and wet and separate child from parent → causes agitation → worsens symptoms
- **Bottom line** - Mist tents should not be used; humidification provides no clinical benefit
