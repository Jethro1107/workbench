# Content Brief — Section 06: Humidified Air

**Lines:** 58–63 (per outline)

## What to capture

- Treatment of croup with humidified air is **NOT effective**, despite its long history of use
- Humidification of air is neither completely benign nor does it improve respiratory distress
- **Evidence**: systematic review of 3 RCTs (135 children with mild-to-moderate croup) found no difference in croup score after humidified air treatment
- Additional RCT (140 children, moderate-to-severe): traditional humidified blow-by oxygen vs 40% vs 100% humidified oxygen (particle size targeting larynx) → no difference in croup score, epinephrine/dexamethasone treatment, or admission/additional medical care
- Traditional humidified blow-by technique did NOT raise humidity above ambient room air (effectively a placebo arm)

## Potential harms of humidified air

- Hot humidified air: scald injuries
- Mist tents: disperse fungus and moulds unless properly cleaned
- Mist tents are cold and wet and separate child from parent → causes agitation → worsens symptoms
- Bottom line: **mist tents should not be used**; humidification provides no benefit

## Suggested sub-bullets

- Historical note: mist rooms and mist tents were standard of care for decades before evidence demonstrated ineffectiveness
- Why it persisted: plausible mechanism, historical tradition; harm seemed minimal
- The evidence is now clear and consistent across multiple RCTs
