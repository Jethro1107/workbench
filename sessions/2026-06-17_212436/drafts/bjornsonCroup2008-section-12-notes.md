# Conclusion

Corticosteroids are firmly established as the treatment of choice for children with croup after 50 years of controversy.

- **Corticosteroids** - firmly established as treatment of choice for children with croup after 50 years of controversy; greatly reduced health-care use and enhanced outcomes.
- **Epinephrine** - sufficient data support its role in short-term symptom relief until corticosteroids take effect (comparatively fewer reports than corticosteroids).
- **Mist (humidified air)** - definitively ineffective after >100 years of use.
- **Heliox** - no new therapeutic interventions on the horizon beyond established treatments.
- **Unexplained mysteries** - several remain unexplained w/ respect to cause and pathophysiology of disease.
- **Future directions** - exploration of unanswered questions could ultimately yield novel and even more effective treatments or vaccines.
