# Section 05 — General Care

## Source
Bjornson & Johnson, Lancet 2008: lines 201–211

---

- **Comfort and minimising agitation** — general consensus that children with croup should be made as comfortable as possible; agitation causes substantial worsening of symptoms:
    - Clinicians should take special care during assessment and treatment not to frighten or upset the child.
    - Sitting the child comfortably in the lap of a parent or caregiver is usually the best way to lessen agitation.48
    - *Evidence base* is primarily consensus rather than RCT data.

- **Oxygen therapy** — no published evidence that oxygen should be administered to children showing signs of respiratory distress, yet widespread consensus indicates it is beneficial in this circumstance;48,63–67:
    - Can generally be administered without causing agitation via a plastic hose with the opening held within a few centimetres of the nose and mouth — referred to as *blow-by oxygen*.48
    - Low-tech and well-accepted approach.

- **Mild cases** — no specific interventions beyond comfort, positioning, and blow-by oxygen are required.
