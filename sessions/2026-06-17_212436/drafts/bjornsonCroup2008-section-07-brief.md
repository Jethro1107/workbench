# Content Brief — Section 07: Heliox

**Lines:** 64–68 (per outline)

## What to capture

- Helium is an inert low-density gas with no pharmacological or biological effects
- Helium-oxygen mixture (heliox) reduces distress in severe respiratory distress by decreasing airflow turbulence through a narrow airway (lower density than nitrogen)
- **Evidence from prospective RCTs**:
  - vs racemic epinephrine: 29 children with moderate-to-severe croup (all received humidified oxygen + IM dexamethasone); both heliox and racemic epinephrine → similar improvements in croup score over time
  - vs oxygen-enriched air: 15 children with mild croup; trend toward greater improvement with heliox but not statistically significant
- **Limitations**:
  - Not shown to offer greater improvements than standard treatments
  - Difficult to use in unskilled hands
  - Limited fractional concentration of inspired oxygen in hypoxic children
  - Practical limitations preclude routine recommendation

## Suggested sub-bullets

- Heliox is a niche intervention for severe cases in skilled settings; not first-line
- Mechanism is physically sound but clinical benefit not demonstrated beyond standard care
- Insufficient evidence to recommend general use in children with severe croup
