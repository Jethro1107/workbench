# Assessment of Severity

- **Severity determined by clinical assessment** — objective methods for assessing respiratory distress are either impractical or insensitive across the full range of disease severity
- **Clinical scores** (e.g., croup scores) are useful in research trials but **none has been shown to enhance routine clinical care**; not reliable when used by a wide range of clinicians
- **Outcome measures in trials** — mainly clinical scores and health-care use
- **Features useful in routine clinical assessment** (per the management algorithm figure, adapted from Johnson D et al., Alberta Medical Association clinical practice guidelines):
    - *Mild* — no stridor or significant chest-wall indrawing at rest → may discharge home
    - *Moderate* — stridor and chest-wall indrawing at rest without agitation
    - *Severe* — stridor and indrawing of the sternum associated with agitation or lethargy

> *n.b.* severity assessment is integral to the treatment algorithm — corticosteroid use, epinephrine, observation duration, and admission decisions all flow from severity classification.
