# Content Brief — Section 05: General Care

**Lines:** 54–57 (per outline)

## What to capture

- Children should be made as comfortable as possible
- Special care: do NOT frighten or upset the child — agitation causes substantial worsening of symptoms
- Best positioning: child sitting comfortably in the lap of a parent or caregiver (minimises agitation)
- **Oxygen**: widespread consensus that oxygen is beneficial in children showing signs of respiratory distress, despite lack of published evidence
- Oxygen can generally be administered without causing agitation via plastic hose with opening held a few centimetres from nose and mouth ("blow-by oxygen")
- No specific "interventions" required beyond comfort and positioning for mild cases

## Suggested sub-bullets

- Evidence base for general care is primarily consensus rather than RCT data
- Key principle: minimise interventions that frighten the child; parenteral presence is therapeutic
- Blow-by oxygen is low-tech and well-accepted
