# Pharmacotherapy — Other Drugs

No controlled trials support use of analgesics/antipyretics, antibiotics, antitussives, decongestants, or short-acting β2 agonists in croup.

- **Analgesics / antipyretics** - Reasonable for reduction of fever or discomfort; no RCT evidence but widely accepted as appropriate symptomatic support.
- **Antibiotics** - Most croup is viral; superinfections (bacterial tracheitis, pneumonia) are rare (<1 per 1000 croup cases), making prophylactic antibiotics unreasonable.
- **Antitussives and decongestants** - No physiologically rational basis exists for use in upper-airway obstruction; should not be administered.
- **Short-acting β2 agonists** - No clear rationale for use in upper-airway disease; croup is not lower-airway bronchospasm; should not be administered.

n.b. Practice variation documented across 21 Alberta hospitals — these unproven drug categories are nonetheless sometimes used despite no evidence.

**Key contrast:** Established therapies (corticosteroids, epinephrine) have robust RCT evidence; these adjunct categories do not.
